
As Jesus approached Bethphage and Bethany on the Mount of Olives, he dispatched two disciples to bring a donkey and a young donkey, instructing them to present them as a sign, fulfilling prophecy. The crowd, witnessing this, spread their cloaks and shouted “Hosanna! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord,” as Jesus entered Jerusalem and began to drive out those conducting commerce in the Temple, overturning their tables and proclaiming the Temple’s intended purpose.  The following day, seeking rest, he retreated to Bethany, where a dinner was prepared in his honor, including the miraculous raising of Lazarus.  As the day drew to a close, a wealthy woman, Mary, poured expensive perfume on Jesus’ feet, a gesture that prompted a debate among his disciples, particularly Judas Iscariot, who plotted to betray him.  The Jewish authorities, aware of the growing crowds and Jesus’s influence, began to conspire to arrest him, but failed to find a legitimate charge.  On the eve of Passover, Jesus’s disciples discussed his impending departure, and he warned them of impending betrayal and hardship.  That night, Judas betrayed Jesus, and the disciples, led by Peter, fled, leaving Jesus to be arrested and subjected to flogging.  He was then brought before Pilate, who, despite lacking evidence of wrongdoing, yielded to the pressure of the crowd and condemned him to crucifixion.  As he died, Jesus’s words fulfilled scripture, and the Temple curtain was torn in two.  Mary Magdalene and the other women went to the tomb, finding it empty, and were met by angels who announced his resurrection.
